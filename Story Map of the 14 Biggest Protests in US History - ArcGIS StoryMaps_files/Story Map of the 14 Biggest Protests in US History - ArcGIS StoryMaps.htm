Story Map of the 14 Biggest Protests in US History

This story map shows and talks about the 14 biggest protests that have happened in US History.

Sindhooja Mullapudi

June 10, 2020

INTRODUCTION

Protests have been happening multiple times through out American history as a way for the people to show their outrage and feelings against hate, inequality, and violence. And most of the protests have led to positive changes in the laws and in the behavior of people. As people residing in this country, we cannot forget that major changes have developed due to protests. An example is the Civil Rights Bills (which you will read more about below) was passed as the effect of a protest. Protests are the foundation that built America, that should never be forgotten. It is important to be educated about certain protests that brought thousands, and even millions, of people together. Those protests show the unity Americans hold in their hearts.

Below is a story map of multiple protests that changed history and perspective in America, in chronological order.

#1 The 1963 Civil Rights March

This march on Washington DC for jobs and freedom was to protest the systemic disenfranchisement of black Americans.

Over 250,000 people participated. It was during this march that MLK Jr. delivered his famous "I Have a Dream" speech.

It was after this march that the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965 were enacted during President Kennedy's administration.

#2 Anti-Vietnam War Protest

This protest in Washington DC was to persuade the Government to stop America's involvement in the Vietnam War. Multiple small protests have been happening for years but this protest was the biggest and one of the more successful ones.

By November 1967 there were over 15,000 US casualties and the War was costing the US over $25 billion per year.

There were about 500,000-600,000 people who participated. This protest is believed to be the largest antiwar protest in US History. As the movement grew, US finally ended its official involvement in the war in 1973.

#3 The Solidarity Day March

This march was to protest President Reagan's budget cuts and tax policies. There were about 260,000 people who participated. And they were protesting Republican policies. Multiple unionists from different sectors joined in and protested for a change.

#4 Anti-Nuclear March

The Anti-Nuclear March took place in New York City's Central Park. The protesters wanted nuclear disarmament by the US Government. This was also during President Regan's term.

There were over a million protesters. The march was filled with peace groups, pacifists, anarchists, children, Buddhist monks, Roman Catholic Bishops, communist party leaders, students, and union leaders. This is the largest nuclear protest and the largest political demonstration in US History.

#5 The Million Man March

This March was to promote unity and family values in black communities. The aim was to unite the black community.

There were over a million people who participated. The march had famous speakers such as Jesse Jackson, Rosa Parks, and Maya Angelou (and many more).

#6 LGBTQ Rights March of 1993

This march was on the National Mall in Washington DC. The participants wanted a civil rights bill against discrimination, an increase in AIDS research funding, and reproductive rights

There were about a million people who participated in the march.

#7 Million Woman March

This march took place in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Over 2 million people participated in the march.

The aim of this march was for unity in the black communities, specifically among black women

#8 Iraq War Protest

Protests happened in all major cities during this day. This protest was to stop US involvement in the War. Though that could not be achieved, the protest was successful in knocking out the political party that started it.

The protests in all the major cities in the US had over 500,000 people participating. But in the coming days, over 10-15 million people marched in 600 cities across the world in 2003.

This protest was during George W. Bush's administration. And it is the biggest known protest in the World History.

#9 March for Women's Lives

The march was on Washington DC. This march was to advocate for reproductive rights, women's rights, and much more.

This march is one of the largest protests in American history. It had over a million people participating in it. And triggered multiple other related marches around the Nation.

#10 The People's Climate March

This march was the biggest call-to-action on climate change. It took place in New York City, and had over 400,000 people participating in it.

This march was the beginning to multiple other marches on climate change and the environment.

This is during Obama's administration.

#11 March for Science

This march was held on Earth Day in all the major cities. The protesters advocated about the increase in global temperatures and protested Trump's budget cuts to the EPA.

This is during Trump's Adminstration.

There were overall, over a million people participating in the marches all over the Nation.

#12 Women's March

This was the largest women's march in US history. The protest called for women's autonomy over their own bodies. This is considered the largest single-day protest in US History.

This is during Trump's adminstration.

The protest had over 5 million people.

#13 March for Our Lives

This march was a student-led protest for stricter gun control laws. The protest happened on Washington DC but triggered multiple protests all over the Nation and in other countries as well.

This is during Trump's administration.

About 2 million people participated in this march.

#14 Black Lives Matter Protest

Thousands of people all across the Nation protested against police brutality and systemic racism and oppression.

This is during Trump's administration.

This movement is still happening as we speak.

CONCLUSION

This story map was made as a way to educate people on the protests that have happened, and where they took place and how the spread.

It is also interesting to see that most of the popular protests (and essentially human rights protests) have been happening during the Trump administration. Coincidence? That is up to everyone to think about.

The 14th protest that has been mentioned is the only protest out of them all that had all 50 states involved. Which is incredible. But what is also interesting is that, the first protest mentioned here (the Civil Rights March) and the last protest (Black Lives Matter March) are about the same thing. This shows that in the last 57 years, barely nothing has changed. But while looking at the two maps, we can also see that back then the march happened only in Washington DC. Now, the protests and marches are happening all over the Nation. Which is a good place to start. This could be because of the increase in awareness, the access to more information, a decrease in the digital divide, or simply more unity. Whatever the reason may be, improvement and changes are happening all over the Nation and the World.
